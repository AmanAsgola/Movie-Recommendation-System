The System

A recommendation engine that understands what you mean, not just what you type.

CineAI combines deep learning, semantic understanding, and 20 million ratings to surface the right film at the right moment — ranked from a catalogue of 27,278 titles, with no shortcuts.

🎯

Personalised, not popular

Every recommendation is ranked against the full 27,278-film catalogue using a learned preference model — not trending lists, not cached results. From 10 suggestions, a user finds 2.5 films they genuinely love. That number started at near-zero and was engineered upward across 20 documented iterations.

🧠

Meaning, not keywords

The content layer runs on Sentence-BERT — a transformer model that encodes plot, cast, director, and genre as a unified 384-dimensional semantic vector. It understands that "psychological thriller" and "dark crime drama" describe the same emotional experience. Keyword matching cannot.

📐

Ranked, not predicted

The collaborative model uses BPR ranking loss — it learns to place films a user loves above films they haven't seen, rather than guessing a star rating. Rating predictions (RMSE 0.7127) come from a separate SVD layer that outperforms the $1M Netflix Prize–winning algorithm by 16.8%.

STARTING POINT Precision@10: 0.005

A Humble Beginning

The first model had a core flaw: it was evaluated against users it had never seen during training. With nearly no overlap between training and test users, recommendations were effectively random noise — a useful reminder that a model is only as good as the pipeline around it.

FIRST BREAKTHROUGH +1,400% ↑ 0.075

Getting the Foundations Right

Rebuilt the evaluation pipeline on scipy SVD, ensured the system only ran against users it had actually learned from, and applied proper Precision@K scoring. A single day of rigorous engineering produced a 1,400% jump — proof that measurement quality matters as much as model quality.

DATA INSIGHT +144% ↑ 0.183

Smarter Data Strategy

Instead of sampling users at random, the model was trained exclusively on the 12,000 most active viewers in the dataset. Users with richer rating histories produce cleaner preference signals. This single data decision — no architecture change — delivered the largest single-step accuracy gain of the project.

RICHER SIGNALS +14% ↑ 0.210

TMDB Metadata Enrichment

Fetched full plot overviews, cast, director, and keywords for all 27,000 movies from the TMDB API. The content layer shifted from 20 bare genre labels to rich, expressive film descriptions. Two-stage retrieval — broad genre similarity first, then detailed re-ranking — made content recommendations meaningfully more relevant.

DEEP LEARNING +19% ↑ · GOOD ✅ 0.250

Neural Collaborative Filtering

Added Neural Collaborative Filtering (NCF/NeuMF, He et al. 2017 WWW Best Paper). A GMF branch captures linear taste patterns; a deep MLP branch captures non-linear ones. Trained with BPR pairwise ranking loss — which directly optimises for top-10 ranking rather than rating accuracy. Accelerated on Apple M5 MPS hardware.

CURRENT STATE +5,034% total ↑ 0.2567 GOOD ✅

Sentence-BERT + NCF + iALS Ensemble

Replaced keyword matching with Sentence-BERT — 384-dimensional semantic embeddings that capture meaning, not just words. "Psychological thriller" and "dark crime drama" now map to neighbouring vectors. The collaborative side runs as a 0.55×NCF + 0.45×iALS rank fusion, where each model corrects the blind spots of the other. The result exceeds the published NCF benchmark.

Technology

Four algorithms.
One recommendation.

⚡

iALS

Implicit Alternating Least Squares. Treats highly-rated movies as strong positive signals. Confidence scales with rating strength — films users loved get pushed to the very top of rankings.

RANKING OPTIMIZED
🧠

NCF/NeuMF

Neural Collaborative Filtering. A GMF branch captures linear taste patterns; an MLP branch captures non-linear ones. Trained with BPR pairwise ranking loss. Accelerated on Apple M5 MPS — 35% faster than CPU.

DEEP LEARNING · MPS
🔤

Sentence-BERT

all-MiniLM-L6-v2. 384-dim semantic embeddings for all 27K movies. Understands meaning — "psychological thriller ≈ dark crime drama". Encoded once at startup and cached to disk.

SEMANTIC AI · TRANSFORMER
🔀

Ensemble Fusion

NCF + iALS rank fusion (0.55×NCF + 0.45×iALS), then hybrid blending with SBERT content (0.70×collab + 0.30×content). Two models correct each other's blind spots — the ensemble consistently outperforms either alone.
